About Me

I am a Doctoral Researcher in the Computer Science Department at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln under the guidance of Dr. Witawas Srisa-an, specializing in Interpretable AI Infrastructure, with extensive experience in cloud engineering, ML infrastructure, and compiler optimizations.

My vision is to create an LLVM-equivalent for AI interpretability - a modular, extensible, production-ready platform that makes neural networks as analyzable and debuggable as traditional software. This will enable safer AI deployment through systematic understanding of model behaviors and failure modes.

IAM Platform - Ph.D. Dissertation

IAM (Interpretable AI with MLIR) is my Ph.D. dissertation project that brings compiler-grade abstractions to neural network analysis. This research demonstrates how treating neural networks as programs with analyzable IR can enable systematic interpretability at scale.

Key Contributions

  • Custom MLIR dialect for interpretability operations
  • Sparse autoencoder/transcoder architecture with TPU optimization
  • Compiler techniques (SSA, taint analysis, slicing) for neural networks
  • End-to-end observability and tracing infrastructure
